--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. OVERALL ASSESSMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Clean, correct sealed base (PrimitiveTuple.java:36-37 permits the 8 primitive
tuple families). The self-type generic <TP extends PrimitiveTuple<TP>> is the
right design and is closed correctly because the type is sealed and every
permitted subtype binds TP to itself. The shared toolkit — arity() [abstract,
44], accept() [56], map() [73, @MayReturnNull], filter() [90], toOptional()
[100] — is minimal and appropriate. Only one cross-class consistency nit.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. NAMING ISSUES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
None.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. RETURN-TYPE AND PARAMETER ISSUES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
accept/map/filter null-callback exception | PrimitiveTuple.java:56,73,90 | Medium
   | DOCUMENT or UNIFY (cross-class consistency). These three dereference the
   functional argument directly, so a null callback yields NullPointerException
   (the Javadoc correctly states @throws NullPointerException). However the
   sibling forEach methods on the concrete tuples validate via
   N.checkArgNotNull(action) and throw IllegalArgumentException (e.g.
   IntTuple.java:754). So a null callback throws NPE through accept/map/filter
   but IAE through forEach. This is the library-wide IAE-vs-NPE-for-null-callback
   split. Low risk; recommend documenting the convention once and applying it
   uniformly, or standardizing on one exception. (Behavior is internally
   consistent and matches its own Javadoc, so this is a consistency item, not a
   bug.)
